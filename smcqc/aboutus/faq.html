FAQs

Basic Education FAQ's

  • What curriculum does SMCQC Basic Education Department offer?
    The Basic Education Department of SMCQC offers K to 12 Curriculum for Kindergarten, Grades 1 to 4 for the Elementary level and Grades 7 to 10 for the Junior High School Level. The Grades 5 and 6 curricula follow the Revised Secondary Education Curriculum (RBEC) of the Department of Education. The department also adheres with the Refined RVM Pedagogy interwoven with the Understanding by Design (UbD) framework which focuses on the acquisition of learning, making meaning and transfer of learning.
  • What is the basis for sectioning?
    For the Elementary level, the sectioning is based on the pupilsâ€™ final grade ranking. The first 80 to 90 pupils in the rank make up the two cream sections and the rest of the pupils are grouped heterogeneously. For the Junior High School level, sectioning is based on 40% weighted average of all the subjects and 60% weighted average of the Mathematics, Science and English grades. The first 80 to 90 students in the rank make up the two cream sections and the rest of the students are grouped heterogeneously.
  • What special academic programs do you offer?
    The department offers programs for both excelling and academically challenged students. In the Elementary level, the department offers special classes in English, Mathematics and Science from Grade 4 to 6 levels. These are the Integrated Class in English (ICE), Advanced Class in Mathematics (ACM) and Intelligence Circle in Science (ICS). The department also offers special programs for academically challenged pupils from Grades 1 to 6. These are the individualized and group enhancement classes and tutorial sessions. In the Junior High School level, the advanced classes are the Mathematics Achievers Course (MAC) and the Science Achievers Program (SAP) that are offered to students from Grades 8 to 10 who excel in these subjects. Enhancement classes in Araling Panlipunan, English, Filipino, Mathematics and Science are offered to students who need further assistance in these subjects. Extension classes in Araling Panlipunan and Filipino are offered to students coming from foreign schools and Extension Classes in Christian Living is offered to students who come from a non-Catholic school without CL subject or its equivalent.
  • What grading system does the department use?
    As per DepEd Order no.8 series of 2015, the department follows the New Policy Guideline in the Grading System. The grading system is as follows:
    AP, English and Filipino Mathematics and Science CL, EEP/TLE, Computer and MAPEH
    Written Works 30% 40% 20%
    Performance Tasks 50% 40% 60%
    Quality Assessment 20% 20% 20%
  • When is the scheduled conference of the parents with the teachers?
    Mid-Quarter Parent-Teacher Conference (PTC) is being held two weeks prior to the scheduled quarterly examinations. This is to help the parents of pupils/students who are at risk of failing to prepare and guide their son/daughter for the remaining subject requirements. Regular PTC is being held three weeks after the scheduled quarterly examinations. Report cards are issued to parents during the regular PTC.
  • Will the department offer Senior High School? If yes, what tracks and strands will you offer?
    Yes. The department will offer Senior High School particularly the Academic Track with all of its four strands: Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM), Accountancy, Business and Management (ABM), Humanities and Social Science (HumSS) and General Academic Strand (GAS).
  • Is there an entrance exam for new students?
    For incoming Kindergarten and Grades 1 to 10, pupils/students are required to take entrance examination. However, there is no entrance examination given to incoming students in the Senior High School.
  • Is there a reservation fee prior to enrollment?
    For incoming Kindergarten and Grades 1 to 10, pupils/students, the reservation fee is Php 1,000.00. For incoming student in the Senior High School, the registration fee is Php 500.00. Such is refundable if the pupil/student decides not to continue his/her enrolment at SMCQC.
